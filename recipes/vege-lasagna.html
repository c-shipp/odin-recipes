Hearty Vegetable Lasagna

You think Lasagna can't taste amazing without meat? I dare you to try this meat-free vegetable lasagna!

Whether you're vegetarian or not, this Hearty Vegetable Lasagna dish is to-die-for. Except you won't be dying for it, because it's an ultra healthy dish that your taste buds will be craving time-and-time again.

Can't get your kids to eat their veggies? Here is your solution, they will be stuffing their faces with this delectable dish, plates cleaned, before you even have to tell them there's no meat. Head to your supermarkets vegetable section, take in all the heart-healthy produce, and let's dive in!

    Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) package lasagna noodles
  • 2 tablespoons avocado oil
  • 1 pound fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • ¾ cup chopped onion
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 (15 ounce) container part-skim ricotta cheese
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

    Directions

  1. Cook lasagna noodles in a large pot of boiling water for 10 minutes, or until al dente. Rinse with cold water, and drain.
  2. Heat oil in a large sauce pan. Add mushrooms, green peppers, onion, and garlic; cook and stir until tender, about 5 minutes. Stir in pasta sauce and basil; bring to a boil. Reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. While the sauce is simmering, mix together ricotta, 2 cups mozzarella cheese, and eggs in a medium bowl.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. Spread 1 cup cooked tomato and vegetable sauce into the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Lay down ½ of the lasagna noodles and layer ½each of the ricotta mix, sauce, and Parmesan cheese on top. Repeat layering again with noodles, ricotta mix, sauce, and Parmesan cheese. Top with remaining 2 cups mozzarella.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven, uncovered, for 40 minutes. Let stand for 15 minutes before serving.
